April 12, 2020

#education #poverty #awareness #eradication

Millions of children around the world remain trapped in child labor. Many of us often ignore problems to the point that they become something normal. Have you ever wondered why a child is working instead of staying at home so that he can go to school. 

There are a lot of children working and taking on roles, responsibilities and duties that they are not supposed to take, but they do it because they have to feed their younger siblings and parents and to meet their financial needs.  As we understand the situation and its impact. A child that is not in the classroom with kids of his age, is missing out on learning and having fun. Improving their social skills and being guided in the way of personal improvement and entrepreneurship. 

The vast majority of child labor is found in rural setting and informal urban economies, children are predominantly employed by their parents rather than factories. Poverty and lack of schools are considered the primary cause of child labor. I'm developing countries with high poverty rate poor schooling opportunities child labor is still prevalent.
